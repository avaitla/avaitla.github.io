Directions Part 3

Welcome Hello There! Welcome to a University of Chicago Architecture Tour. We have a lot of cool stuff on campus, so for the unfamiliar this is a great introduction to the rich architecture of the University. There are three parts, and you will need to go back to the main page (arcengine.com), to choose each one. It's best to follow along by walking it in the real world, but if you aren't from the University you can still take the tour thanks to the power of technology! Just use Google's awesome street view to move through the map! You can hover over markers on the map or on the right hand pane to get more information about the exhibit indicated. In order to see high quality pictures simply click on the marker or the item in the right hand pane. If you want specific directions on how to take the route, simply click on the 'Directions' button in the right hand pane! In addition, a campus map can be found here: maps.uchicago.edu/campus.shtml. Happy Travels!

Exhibit 1
I Beam
5811 S. Maryland Ave., Chicago, Illinois 60637
I beams are metal beams with an `I` shaped cross section. The shape is often used as it is very efficient at handling the various forces involved in support structures.
Many I beams can be found around the bridges of the University Hospital.
Exhibit 2
Metal Sculpture
920 East 58th St., Chicago, Illinois 60637
This metal piece is located directly outside the Life Sciences Building near the street entrance.
Exhibit 3
Arch Construction
John Crerar Library, Chicago, IL 60637
There is quite a notable arch located outside the John Crerar Library. The library is houses many resources on science and science related disciplines. The listed address is 5730 South Ellis Ave.
Exhibit 4
Pilaster
John Crerar Library, Chicago, Illinois 60637
Pilasters are columns which project slightly from a building and are solely decorative. There often frame doorways or windows.
A clear example of a Pilaster can be found outside this building. The doorway faces the John Crerar Library, not 58th street. The listed address is 956 E. 58th St.
Exhibit 5
Round Arch
970 East 58th St., Chicago, IL 60637
The University Bookstore is a great piece of architecture which exhibits three round arches right at its entrance.
Exhibit 6
Ribbed Arch
Bond Chapel University of Chicago, Chicago, IL 60637
Ribbed arches differ from traditional arches, as they have vertical segments going between them. This is feature seen in many Gothic Architecture.
