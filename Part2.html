We have a lot of cool stuff on campus, so for the unfamiliar this is a great introduction to the rich architecture of the University. It's best to follow along by walking it in the real world, but if you aren't from the University you can still take the tour thanks to the power of technology! Just use Google's awesome street view to move through the map! You can hover over markers on the map or on the right hand pane to get more information about the exhibit indicated. In order to see high quality pictures simply click on the marker or the item in the right hand pane. If you want specific directions on how to take the route, simply click on the 'Directions' button in the right hand pane! In addition, a campus map can be found here: maps.uchicago.edu/campus.shtml. Happy Travels!

Exhibit 1
Cantilever Construction
5757 S. Woodlawn Ave, Chicago, Illinois 60637
A cantilever is a beam that overhangs on one end. Careful calculations need to be done in order to verify that the stress from gravity can be counteracted by the anchoring point of the beam. When achieved successfully it produces the nice effect of a free hanging feature.
This building is Frank Lloyd Wrights famous Robie House demonstrating an architectural style known as `prairie style`. It can be found on the corner of Woodlawn and 58th St.
Exhibit 2
Supporting Piers
5807 S. Woodlawn, Chicago, Illinois 60637
Supporting piers are structures that are used for structural support such as load distribution on various structures from bridges to arches.
Right next to the Robie House is the Booth School of Business, which has a cantilever entrance as well. However in order to see the immense supporting piers, one needs to walk inside and stand in the main lobby. They are quite enormous, and the view on a sunny day is breathtaking.
Exhibit 3
Wall Mural
1212 E. 59th St., Chicago, Illinois 60637
Ida Noyes is quite a beautiful building. However, you need to go inside in order to fully appreciate all that it has to offer. The ceilings have exceptional designs and the wooden structures were created with a great deal of care and precision. In addition you may be able to catch a Movie in the Ida Theater when you're there!
On the top floor of Ida there is a grand lobby which contains a beautiful mural that spans the lengths of both walls. It appears to be some sort of Wedding Procession.
Exhibit 4
Wood Sculpture
5850 S. Woodlawn Ave., Chicago, Illinois 60637
The Rockefeller Cathedral is a tribute to great architecture. It has a number of stone sculptures, barreled vaults, and monolithic arches.
Among all this however, if one proceeds to the very center of the Cathedral, you can find three wooden sculptures surrounding the organ.
Exhibit 5
Barrel Vault
William Rainey Harper Memorial Library (1116 E. 59th St) University of Chicago, Chicago, IL 60637
A barrel vault is an architectural feature formed when a single curve is extruded along a given distance.
There is a barrel vault located at the east tunnel of Harper Memorial (this is next to Swift Hall).
Exhibit 6
Bronze Sculpture
William Rainey Harper Memorial Library (1116 E. 59th St) University of Chicago, Chicago, IL 60637
Carl Linne was the Swedish naturalist who outlined the system of Natural Classification (Homo Sapiens). He is known as the father of modern taxonomy as well as one of the founders of modern ecology.
There is a significant statue depicting Carl Linne directly outside of the 59th st. entrance to Harper Memorial.
Exhibit 7
Post and Lintel Construction
6031 S. Ellis Ave., Chicago, Illinois 60637
Post and Lintel Construction is the simple method of using two vertical posts to support a header (lintel).
This type of construction is used to construct the entry walkway to South Campus Residential Commons.
